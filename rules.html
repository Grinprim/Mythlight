Introduction

Mythlight is designed to be a simple narrative focused roleplaying game, with simple to pick up rules that promote roleplay. Combat is also quite simple to pick up using as little rolls as possible. The system has no core stories, having a very open ruleset allowing both high fantasy and low fantasy campaigns.

02 Game Mechanics

The Action Check

Whenever you attempt a risky action, roll 2d6 + Stat. To succeed, your total must meet or exceed the Challenge Score (CS). If you are trained in a specific skill relevant to the check, you may add your Training Bonus (standard +2) to the roll.

The Four Stats

  • Strength: Used for checks involving physical power, health, and melee.
  • Dexterity: Used for speed, stealth, and ranged attacks.
  • Scholarship: Used for knowledge, magic, and medicine.
  • Charisma: Used for social influence and leadership.

Standard Challenge Scores (CS)

The CS represents the difficulty of an action. Typical values include:

5 Easy
8 Moderate
11 Difficult
14+ Epic

03 Combat & Conflict

The Combat Sequence

Characters stats are determined by their component traits. Combat is streamlined to use as few rolls as possible.

  1. Initiative: Determined by rolling 2d6 + Dexterity. Characters act in order from highest result to lowest.
  2. Movement & Action: On your turn, you may move and perform one major action (such as an attack or casting a spell).
  3. The Attack Check: Roll 2d6 + Stat (Strength for Melee, Dexterity for Ranged). If the total meets or exceeds the target's Defense Score, the attack hits.
  4. Damage Calculation: Damage is calculated as Weapon Damage Score - Target's Toughness. The remaining value is subtracted from the target's Health.

Advantage & Disadvantage

Advantage: Roll 3d6 and drop the lowest result.
Disadvantage: Roll 3d6 and drop the highest result.

Health & Death

When you reach 0 HP, you become unconscious. You must be stabilized within 3 rounds by an ally, or you will perish.

04 Magic & Mana

Magic is granted through specific traits, which are mutually exclusive. You have one central pool of mana. The amount spent corresponds to the spell's power or the upcast cost.

  • Learning Spells: You gain spells equal to 1 per level + your Scholarship modifier.
  • Upcasting: Cast a spell with a level higher than its base to enhance effects. Cost: +1 mana per level above base.
  • Cantrips: Special spells that cost no mana and can be cast freely.

05 Equipment & Arms

Weapon Tags

  • Light: Weapons that are easier to handle. These gain a +2 to hit, but deal 1 less damage.
  • Heavy: Large, cumbersome weapons. These have -2 to hit, but deal 1 extra damage.
  • Magic: Infused with arcane power. These use Scholarship instead of Strength or Dexterity for attack rolls.

Armor & Defense

Armor provides Toughness, which acts as a flat reduction to incoming damage scores.

No Armor 0 Toughness
Light Armor 1 Toughness
Heavy Armor 2 Toughness
Shields +1 Defense Score

06 Recovery & Rests

Short Rest

A brief pause in the action to catch your breath. Some traits may allow for partial mana recovery or minor healing during these periods.

Sleeping (Long Rest)

Mana is fully restored when you sleep. After sleeping, you may swap out prepared spells for others available to your spellcasting type, provided they are within your level limit. This also restores full Health.
