Introduction

Mythlight is a narrative-focused roleplaying game designed to be simple to pick up while allowing for deep, open-ended storytelling. Whether you are traversing high-fantasy kingdoms or gritty, low-magic landscapes, the rules remain consistent.

Requires 2d6 Dice
1 Narrator & Players

01 Core Mechanics

The Action Check

Whenever you attempt a risky action, roll 2d6 + Attribute Modifiers. To succeed, your total must meet or exceed the Challenge Score (CS) set by the Narrator.

Attributes

  • Strength: Physical power, health, and melee.
  • Dexterity: Speed, stealth, and ranged attacks.
  • Scholarship: Knowledge, magic, and medicine.
  • Charisma: Social influence and leadership.

Standard Challenge Scores (CS)

5 Easy
8 Moderate

14+ Epic

02 Combat & Conflict

The Combat Sequence

  1. Initiative: Roll 2d6 + Dexterity. Highest goes first.
  2. The Attack: Roll 2d6 + Stat (Melee: Strength | Ranged: Dexterity). Success occurs if the roll exceeds the target's Defense Score.
  3. Dealing Damage: Subtract the target's Toughness (from Armor) from your weapon's Damage Score. The remainder is dealt to the target's HP.

Advantage & Disadvantage

When you have Advantage, roll 3d6 and drop the lowest. When you have Disadvantage, roll 3d6 and drop the highest result.

Dying Status

When reduced to 0 HP, you become Unconscious. If not stabilized within 3 rounds, the character dies.

03 Magic & Mana

Magic in Mythlight is fueled by Mana. Your Mana pool grows as you level up, allowing you to cast more powerful spells or "upcast" lower-level spells for +1 Mana per level to enhance their effects.

Spell Level Thresholds

  • Level 1 Spells Available at Level 1
  • Level 2 Spells Unlocked at Level 3
  • Level 3 Spells Unlocked at Level 6
  • Level 4 Spells Unlocked at Level 9
Note: You gain 1 spell per level + your Scholarship modifier. Cantrips cost 0 mana.

04 Equipment & Arms

Weapon Properties

  • Light: +2 to hit, but deals 1 less damage.
  • Heavy: -2 to hit, but deals 1 extra damage.
  • Magic: Uses Scholarship instead of Strength/Dex for attack rolls.

Armor & Toughness

Armor provides Toughness, which is a flat reduction to incoming damage.

No Armor 0 Toughness
Light Armor 1 Toughness
Heavy Armor 2 Toughness

05 Recovery & Rests

Short Rest

A 1-hour pause to bind wounds and catch breath. You do not recover mana unless a specific trait (like Arcane Recovery) allows it. Some traits allow for minor health recovery during this time.

Long Rest (Sleep)

A minimum of 6 hours of sleep. Provides full recovery of HP and Mana. After a long rest, you may swap your Prepared Spells for any others available to your spellcasting type.
