Core Rules

Mythlight is a narrative-focused roleplaying system designed for fast resolution, minimal dice complexity, and flexible storytelling. The rules exist to provide consistency and fairness, but the Narrator is always encouraged to prioritize pacing, tone, and story impact. Characters are defined by traits, choices, and narrative consequences rather than rigid class structure.

01 The 2d6 Core Engine

All uncertain actions in Mythlight are resolved using a 2d6 system. Roll two six-sided dice and apply all relevant modifiers.

Roll Formula: 2d6 + Attribute + Skill Modifiers + Situational Modifiers

  • Success: Total meets or exceeds the Challenge Score (CS).
  • Failure: Total is below the CS; the Narrator introduces complications.

Advantage: Roll an additional d6. There is no maximum.
Disadvantage: Remove one d6. Dice pools may reach 0d6, but never below.

Midnight (Critical Success): Rolling two natural sixes on an attack automatically hits and deals double damage. Additional sixes increase the multiplier.

Snake Eyes (Critical Failure): Rolling two natural ones on an attack automatically misses and causes a severe complication.

02 Attributes & Skills

Characters are defined by four core attributes. Attributes influence skill checks, combat rolls, survivability, and social presence.

Physique

Strength, endurance, and physical resilience. Governs Might, Athletics, Fortitude, and Brawn.

Technique

Precision and coordination. Governs Aim, Finesse, Reflex, and Tinkering.

Mystique

Arcane knowledge and willpower. Governs Arcana, Spellcraft, Insight, and Scholarship.

Charm

Social presence and reputation. Charm starts at 0 and changes dynamically through play.

03 Combat & Actions

Action Economy

Combat occurs in Scenes lasting six seconds. Each participant takes one Turn per Scene. On your Turn, you may take two Actions. You may also take one Reaction per Scene.

Health & Death

Health is tracked as Hits. At 0 Hits you fall unconscious. Enemies may declare final blows. Massive damage equal to your maximum health below zero results in instant death.

04 Magic & Mana

Spellcasting is granted through specific traits and draws from a single central Mana pool. Spellcasting traits are mutually exclusive unless otherwise stated.

Mana is restored when you sleep. Some traits allow limited recovery during rests. Cantrips cost no Mana and may be cast freely.

Spell levels unlock at specific character levels: Level 1 (creation), Level 2 (3), Level 3 (6), Level 4 (9).

Mana progression increases with level, reaching a maximum of 16 at level 12.

05 Recovery & Progression

Rests & Sleep

Rests restore limited health based on level. Sleep fully restores Health and Mana and removes Fatigue.
