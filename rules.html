Core Rules

The fundamental mechanics that govern the Mythlight system.

Introduction

Mythlight is designed to be a simple narrative focused roleplaying game, with simple to pick up rules that promote roleplay. Combat is also quite simple to pick up using as little rolls as possible.

What you need to play:

  • 2 six-sided dice (2d6) per player. This system doesn’t use polyhedral dice.
  • Character Sheet: Includes stats, traits, equipment, and backstory.
  • One Narrator: Guides the story, plays NPCs, and presents challenges.
01 Resolution System

The 2d6 Engine

Most actions use a base roll of 2d6 to determine success or failure. You succeed if your total (Roll + Modifiers) meets or beats the CS (Challenge Score).

Advantage Roll one additional d6 (3d6 total). Keep all. No upper limit on added dice.
Disadvantage Remove one d6 (1d6 total). You may reduce pool to 0d6.

Midnight (Critical Success)

Triggered only on two natural sixes.

  • Automatically hits.
  • Deals Double Damage.
  • With Advantage: 3+ sixes increases multiplier.

Snake Eyes (Critical Failure)

Triggered on two natural ones.

  • Automatically misses.
  • Causes a critical failure effect (tripping, self-damage).
  • Snake Eyes cannot occur when rolling 0d6.
02 Time & Rests

The Scene

Lasts 6 seconds. One full cycle of every participant taking their Turn.

Rest (>10 mins)

Regain Hits equal to Level. Longer rests stack (1hr = 2x Level). Max 2 rests per day.

Sleep (8 hrs)

Full recovery of HP and Mana. Removes Fatigue. Sleeping less avoids exhaustion but may not restore resources.

Fatigue: -2 penalty per level (stacking). Level 6 without sleep is fatal.

03 Character Creation

Attributes (Point Buy)

You have 4 points to distribute. Max 2 points per stat initially. These are modified by Lineage.

Physique Physical strength, agility, endurance.
Mystique Magical knowledge, control, potency.
Technique Engineering, tinkering, fine manual skill.

Note: Charm is determined by gameplay, starting at 0.

04 Skills & Actions

Each attribute governs specific skills. Skill Checks follow: [2d6 + Attribute + Skill Modifier].

Physique Skills

  • Might: Lifting, breaking, grappling.
  • Athletics: Running, jumping, climbing.
  • Fortitude: Resisting poison, pain, disease.
  • Brawn: Physical resistance (HP).

Technique Skills

  • Tinkering: Repairing, crafting, traps.
  • Finesse: Sleight of hand, picking locks.
  • Aim: Ranged accuracy.
  • Reflex: Dodging, reacting.

Mystique Skills

  • Arcana: Spells, rituals, lore.
  • Insight: Reading intentions, sensing magic.
  • Scholarship: Research, history, ID.
  • Spellcraft: Casting and shaping magic.

Charm (Social)

Starts at 0. Range: -4 to 4.

  • Persuasion: Negotiating, rallying.
  • Deception: Lying, bluffing.
  • Performance: Entertaining.
  • Intimidation: Coercing through fear.

Grace Mechanics

You acquire grace as you fail. Use it in dire moments.

  • Gain: +1 Grace on failure. +1 per subsequent consecutive failure.
  • Delay: Success does not reset Grace, but delays growth by 1 (must fail twice to grow again).
  • Spend: Must spend ALL Grace to boost any roll. Max Grace is 6.
  • Reset: Resets when combat starts/ends or on sleep.
05 Progression
  • Leveling (Max 12) Milestone based. Gain 1 Trait Point per level (2 points at levels 3, 6, 9, 12).
  • Health Calculation HP = Level + (Brawn Modifier × Level)
  • Traits Purchased with points. Can be mutually exclusive. Level 1 starts with 3 Trait Points.
06 Spellcasting

Magic draws from a central pool of Mana. Mana restores on Sleep.

Upcasting

Cast a spell at a higher level for enhanced effects. Cost: +1 Mana per level.

Cantrips

Cost 0 Mana. Cast freely.

07 Equipment

Encumbrance Limit = Physique + (2 × Athletics Total)

Item Weights: None (0), Light (1), Medium (2), Heavy (3), Cumbersome (4). 100 coins = 1 weight.

Encumbered (> Limit) Speed halved. 0 move = disadvantage.
Overencumbered (> 2x Limit) Speed halved. Disadv on Physique/Technique.
Crushed (> 3x Limit) Speed 0. Disadv all. 1 dmg/10min.
08 Combat Mechanics

The Turn Structure

Initiative: Determined by word of mouth. First to engage acts first. Unconscious = bottom of order.

NPC Initiative: NPCs have a Turn Delay based on when they joined combat.

Action Economy
Each Turn = 2 Actions.
Each Scene = 1 Reaction (Only valid reaction by default is Opportunity Attack leaving melee).

Attack Formulas

Melee Attack
2d6 + Physique + Might Mod + Weapon Mod + Accuracy
Ranged Attack
2d6 + Technique + Aim Mod + Weapon Mod + Accuracy
Magic Attack
2d6 + Mystique + Spellcraft Mod + Focus Mod + Accuracy

*Off-hand attacks use ONLY base stat score.
*Finesse weapons use Technique + Finesse.
*Improvised weapons use only Might (melee) or Aim (thrown) modifiers.

09 Health & Death

Armor & Guard

Unarmored Defense: 6 + Fortitude Modifier.

Guard: Temporary HP. Always lost before Health. Cannot be regained until sleep.

Death

0 Hits: Unconscious.

Final Blow: Attacker declares on unconscious target. Instantly kills unless resisted by Fortitude (up to 3 times).

Certain Death: Negative HP equal to Max HP kills instantly.

10 Conditions
Bleeding: Lose 1 Health end of turn.
Stunned: Lose 1 action.
Prone: Move halved. Melee hits against you have adv.
Restrained: No move. Attacks against you have adv.
Exhausted: Disadv physical checks. Move halved.
Weakened: Attacks deal half damage.
Blinded: Auto-fail sight checks.
Slowed: Move halved. No reactions.
11 Cover & Terrain

Cover

  • Partial: -1 to -3 to ranged attacks.
  • Half: Disadvantage on attack.
  • Full: Attack cannot succeed.

Terrain

  • Rough: Movement halved.
  • Slippery: Cardinal movement only. Turns trigger Reflex check or Prone.
Ranged Logic: Impossible to shoot beyond max range. Disadvantage if enemy is within melee range.
