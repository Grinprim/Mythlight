Core Rules

Mythlight is a narrative-first RPG. While these rules provide a framework for conflict, the ultimate goal is to tell a compelling story. If a rule gets in the way of a great moment, let the story win.

01 The 2d6 Core Engine

2d6 + Stat
The Universal Roll

How to Perform a Check

When you attempt an action with an uncertain outcome, roll 2 six-sided dice and add your relevant Attribute or Skill bonus.

  • ● Success: If your total equals or exceeds the Challenge Score (CS).
  • ● Midnight: Rolling a Natural 12 usually results in an extraordinary outcome.
  • ● Failure: If your total is lower than the CS, the Narrator describes the complication.
  • ● Snake eyes: Rolling a Natural 2 usually results in an extraordinary failure.

02 Core Attributes

Your character is defined by 4 primary attributes, these determine your characters overall skill in any given area. You are given 5 points to distribute, and are not allowed to put more than 3 in any given stat.

Think about what your character would be good at, and what your goal is to be able to play, and assign the points accordingly!

Physique

Raw strength, physical endurance, and martial power. Used Melee combat, carrying capacity and other physical characteristics.

  • ● Might:
    Raw physical strength for lifting, breaking, grappling, pushing/pulling, or displays of force.
  • ● Athletics:
    Running, jumping, climbing, swimming, and other feats of agility and stamina. In addition it grants carrying capacity.
  • ● Brawn:
    Physical resistance, corresponding to the character's hp, higher brawn is higher hp.
  • ● Fortitude:
    Resisting poison, disease, exhaustion, extreme weather, or pain. Used for un-armored defence.

Technique

A mix between hand-eye co-ordination and reflexes, aswell as technical knowladge like assembly and modificatio of machines.

  • ● Aim:
    Accuracy with ranged attacks, throwing objects, or hitting small targets.
  • ● Finesse:
    Sleight of hand, picking locks, balancing, or using small tools with precision, looting.
  • ● Tinkering:
    Repairing, crafting, disarming traps, and manipulating delicate mechanisms.
  • ● Reflex:
    Dodging attacks, reacting to sudden events, catching thrown objects mid-air.

Mystique

Knowladge of the arcane arts, magic and all other mystical understandings, aswell as insight into the world itself.

  • ● Spellcraft:
    Actively shaping, controlling, or modifying magical effects. Used primarly for spellcasting.
  • ● Insight:
    Reading intentions, detecting lies, sensing emotional states, or feeling magical disturbances, looking for clues.
  • ● Arcana:
    Understanding spells, magical creatures, rituals, enchantments, and magical lore.
  • ● Scholarship:
    Researching, recalling historical or scientific facts, identifying creatures and plants.

Charm

Covers all social interactions, from intimidation to persuasion, it is your characters ability outwardly present themselves.

  • ● Persuasion:
    Convincing, negotiating, rallying allies, or inspiring cooperation.
  • ● Deception:
    Lying, bluffing, disguising motives, or concealing the truth.
  • ● Performance:
    Entertaining, public speaking, singing, dancing, storytelling, or dramatic displays.
  • ● Intimidation:
    Coercing others through threats, fear, or sheer presence.

03 Species & Path

Species

Your Species corresponds to your character's physical characteristics as well as cultural upbringing, they provide different benefits and hindrances. Your character's species will act as one of their most recognizable features.

Species provide bonuses to your base attributes, these are minor bonuses, as most are balanced to be equally strong, though some do fit more specific niches. Lastly they provide 1 trait, this corresponds to your species most expected capabilities.

Paths

Your characters past is primarily governed by their backstory, however, mechanically, you do get to select a path for your character. Paths grant you a couple skill modifiers and 1 trait.

Paths can be omitted, to instead grant the necessary trait and modifiers based on the characters backstory. This should only be done with supervision of the Narrator.

04 Actions & Health

Action Economy

On your turn you you have 2 Actions which can be used however you wish, though be careful as you can only make 1 attack per turn!

Health & Dying

When your HP hits 0, you are Downed. You cannot act and must be stabilized by an ally.

04 Magic & Mana

Spellcasting is a specialized skill granted through specific traits. All magic users draw from a central pool of Mana.

Casting Spells

Spending Mana allows you to manifest powerful effects. Higher-level spells cost more Mana, but you can "Upcast" a lower spell to increase its power at +1 Mana per level.

Cantrips

Minor miracles that cost 0 Mana. You can cast these as often as you like, assuming you have the proper trait.

05 Recovery & Rests

Short Rest

Catch your breath. Some traits allow for minor recovery of Mana or health during these brief pauses.

Long Rest (Sleep)

Full recovery of Mana and Health. This is also when you can swap out your Prepared Spells.
