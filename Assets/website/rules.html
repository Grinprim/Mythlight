Core Rules

The fundamental mechanics that govern the Mythlight system.

Introduction

Mythlight is designed to be a simple narrative focused roleplaying game, with simple to pick up rules that promote roleplay. Combat is also quite simple to pick up using as little rolls as possible.

What you need to play:

  • 2 six-sided dice (2d6) per player. This system doesn’t use polyhedral dice.
  • Character Sheet: Includes stats, traits, equipment, and backstory.
  • One Narrator: Guides the story, plays NPCs, and presents challenges.
01 Resolution System

The 2d6 Engine

Most actions use a base roll of 2d6 to determine success or failure. You succeed if your total (Roll + Modifiers) meets or beats the CS (Challenge Score).

Advantage Roll one additional d6 (3d6 total). Keep all. No upper limit on added dice.
Disadvantage Remove one d6 (1d6 total). You may reduce pool to 0d6. In addition you loose the ability to Midnight.
+ Stat vs Challenge Score

Midnight (Critical Success)

Triggered only on two natural sixes.

  • Automatically hits.
  • Deals Double Damage.
  • With Advantage: The trigger remains the same, however any additional rolled sixes will increase the modifier. (ex. 3 sixes = 3x Damage)

Snake Eyes (Critical Failure)

Triggered on two natural ones.

  • Automatically misses.
  • Causes a critical failure effect (tripping, self-damage).
  • Snake Eyes cannot occur when rolling 0d6.

Grace Mechanics

You acquire grace as you fail. Use it in dire moments.

  • Gain: +1 Grace on failure.
  • Delay: Success does not reset Grace, but delays growth by 1 (must fail twice to grow again).
  • Spend: Must spend ALL Grace to boost any roll. Max Grace is 6.
  • Reset: Resets when combat starts/ends or on sleep.
02 Time & Rests

The Scene

Lasts 6 seconds. One full cycle of every participant taking their Turn.

Rest (>10 mins)

Regain Health equal to your Level. Longer rests will heal more, equalling your Level for each Hour rested. Max 2 rests per day.

Sleep (8 hrs)

Full recovery of HP and Mana. Removes Fatigue. Sleeping less avoids exhaustion but may not restore resources.

Fatigue: -2 penalty per level (stacking). Level 6 without sleep is fatal.

Character Rules

These rules govern character creation and how each part of your character functions.

03 Core Attributes + Species & Path

Attributes (Point Buy)

You have 5 points to distribute. Max 2 points per stat initially. These are modified by Lineage.

Physique Physical strength, agility, endurance.
Mystique Magical knowledge, control, potency.
Technique Reflexes, tinkering, fine manual skill.
Charm Conversation skills, persuasion, social understanding.

Species & Path

Your character is more than just numbers, its also your past, culture and appearance! Use these to dictate what those are like:

Species Species represent your character’s physical traits and cultural background, making them one of the most recognizable aspects of who they are. Each species grants minor bonuses to base attributes—balanced overall, though some favor specific playstyles—and provides one unique trait that reflects the species’ most characteristic abilities.
Path Your character's past is primarily governed by their backstory, however, mechanically, you do get to select a path for your character. Paths grant you a couple skill modifiers and 1 trait.
04 Skills

Each attribute governs specific skills, these can be enhanced using skill points. At character creation you are given 4 skill points, you can asign these however you wish, but only a max of 2 in a single skill. Skill Checks follow: [2d6 + Attribute + Skill Modifier].

Physique Skills

  • Might: Lifting, breaking, melee strikes.
  • Athletics: Running, jumping, climbing, grappling, carrying capacity.
  • Fortitude: Resisting poison, pain, disease, un-armored defence.
  • Brawn: Physical resistance (HP).

Technique Skills

  • Tinkering: Repairing, crafting, traps.
  • Finesse: Sleight of hand, picking locks.
  • Aim: Ranged accuracy.
  • Reflex: Dodging, reacting.

Mystique Skills

  • Arcana: Spells, rituals, lore.
  • Insight: Reading intentions, sensing magic.
  • Scholarship: Research, history, number of known spells.
  • Spellcraft: Casting and shaping magic.

Charm (Social)

  • Persuasion: Negotiating, rallying.
  • Deception: Lying, bluffing.
  • Performance: Entertaining.
  • Intimidation: Coercing through fear.
05 Health & Guard

Health represents a creature’s ability to remain functional in combat. It does not represent vitality alone — injuries, exhaustion, and strain are all represented abstractly.

Health

When Health reaches 0, the creature is incapacitated.

For players health is determined based on this formula: HP = Level + (Brawn Modifier × Level)

Guard

Temporary HP. Always lost before Health. Cannot be healed in any way once lost. Only gainable through traits or if given by others.

Death

0 Health: Unconscious.

Final Blow: Attacker declares on unconscious target. Instantly kills unless resisted by Fortitude (up to 4 times).

Certain Death: Negative HP equal to Max HP kills instantly.

07 Spellcasting

Spellcasting Schools

Arcane
Utility-focused spellcasting emphasizing protection, control, and flexible magical effects. Offers the widest range of spells with no inherent restrictions.
Divine
Focuses on healing, blessings, and radiant power. Excels at support, recovery, and sustained protection through sacred magic.
Occult
Cannot learn spells above Level 2. All spells are always cast at the highest spell level currently unlocked. Powerful but tightly constrained.
Cosmic
Cannot learn spells above Level 1. Instead of upcasting, spending N mana casts N Level 1 spells simultaneously (e.g., 3 mana = 3 spells). Manipulates abstract forces such as space, time, and probability.
Elemental
Draws from multiple elemental sub-schools (fire, ice, lightning, earth, and more). Each element alters spell behavior and excels in different environments.

Mana Growth

Magic draws from a central pool of Mana. Mana restores on Sleep.

Upcasting

Cast a spell at a higher level for enhanced effects. Cost: +1 Mana per level.

Cantrips

Cost 0 Mana. Cast freely.

08 Equipment & Encumberance

Armor & Weaponry + Proficiencies

You are restricted in how much of each type of equipment you can wear, on your character sheet you will see sections for what types, 1 peice of equipment per section (helmet, body, shield etc.)

Armor Worn to gain extra defence, usually overrides unarmored defence [5 + Fortitude]
Weaponry You can weild as many weapons as you have arms, although without traits only 1 weapon effectively
Proficiencies You gain the ability to effectively use certain gear from traits
Automatic Proficiencies Light melee weapons, light armor and shields are all available for free.

Encumbrance Limit = Physique + (2 × Athletics Total)

Item Weights: None (0), Light (1), Medium (2), Heavy (3), Cumbersome (4). 100 coins = 1 weight.

Non-encumbered (< Limit) Buisness as usual
Encumbered (> Limit) Speed halved. 0 move = disadvantage.
Overencumbered (> 2x Limit) Speed halved. Disadv on Physique/Technique.
Crushed (> 3x Limit) Speed 0. Disadv all. 1 dmg/10min.

Combat Rules

These rules govern combat and all of its sub-systems.

Movement & Units

Combat takes place on a grid-based map. Becouse of this movement is also declared on the grid as stated bellow. To know how much you can move look at the Speed stat on your character sheet.

  • Each Tile = 1 Unit of Speed
  • Movement is limited too tiles directly adjacent to one another.
  • Diagonal movement does not skip over tiles, you must draw a "staircase" to the tile you want to go to.

Ranged Combat

  • Max Range: Attacks beyond range automatically fail
  • Melee Threat: Ranged attacks while adjacent to an enemy suffer Disadvantage
  • Line of Sight: Solid obstruction blocks ranged attacks
  • Elevation: May negate cover (Narrator ruling)
09 Combat Mechanics

The Turn Structure

Initiative: Determined by word of mouth. First to engage acts first. Unconscious = bottom of order.

NPC Initiative: NPCs have a Turn Delay based on when they joined combat.

Ambush: When you initiate combat with an unaware enemy, you act before initiative or turn delay

Action Economy
Each Turn = 2 Actions. Only 1 Attack per turn (Some traits bypass.)
Each Scene = 1 Reaction (Only valid reaction by default is Opportunity Attack leaving melee).

Attack Formulas

Melee Attack
2d6 + Physique + Might Mod + Weapon Mod + Accuracy
Ranged Attack
2d6 + Technique + Aim Mod + Weapon Mod + Accuracy
Magic Attack
2d6 + Mystique + Spellcraft Mod + Focus Mod + Accuracy

*Off-hand attacks use ONLY base stat score.
*Finesse weapons use Technique + Finesse.
*Improvised weapons use only Might (melee) or Aim (thrown) modifiers.

10 Conditions

Conditions

Conditions represent ongoing states. Some stack, others persist until removed.

Poison

Stacks. Each stack deals damage at turn start.

Bleeding

Stacks. Reduces healing by stack value.

Slow

Stacks. −1 Unit movement per stack.

Stunned

Lose 1 Action.

Prone

No movement. Melee attacks gain Advantage.

Restrained

No movement. Attacks against you gain Advantage.

Exhausted

Disadvantage on physical checks.

Weakened

Deal half damage.

Blinded

Auto-fail sight checks.

Removal
Conditions may require checks, items, magic, or rest. Multiple stacks may be removed at once.
12 Cover & Terrain

Cover

  • Partial: -1 to -3 to ranged attacks.
  • Half: Disadvantage on attack.
  • Full: Attack cannot succeed.

Terrain

  • Rough: Movement halved.
  • Slippery: Cardinal movement only. Turns trigger Reflex check or Prone.
Ranged Logic: Impossible to shoot beyond max range. Disadvantage if enemy is within melee range.

Expanded Conditions

Poison

Stacks in severity. Each stack increases damage per turn.

Removed via antidotes, rest, or medicine.

Bleed

Stacks apply ongoing physical damage.

Can be staunched with an action.

Slow

Each stack reduces movement by 1 Unit.

Ends when stacks reach zero.

Ending conditions in combat usually costs an Action or requires a successful check.

Mounted Combat

  • Mounts act on their rider’s initiative
  • Movement uses the mount’s speed
  • Mounted characters gain advantage on movement-based checks
  • Forced dismounts trigger Reflex checks

Companion Rules

  • Companions act after their controller
  • Commands cost a Bonus or Action
  • Uncommanded companions act defensively
  • Simple stat blocks — no skills required

Cover Rules

Half Cover: +1 Defense
Full Cover: Cannot be targeted
Improvised Cover: Narrator ruling

Characters may create cover using objects, terrain, or magic.

Actions & Reactions

  • 1 Action per turn
  • 1 Bonus (if available)
  • 1 Reaction per round
  • Opportunity Attacks trigger on careless movement

Rituals & Creation

Rituals, crafting, brewing, and smithing are long-form actions that prioritize preparation over speed.

  • Require time, materials, and space
  • Failure has narrative consequences
  • Success may create lasting effects

Narrator Guidance

Creating Creatures
  • Pick 3–5 stats
  • No skills needed
  • 1–3 defining traits
Challenge Score (CS)
  • CS = threat, not HP sponge
  • Match party resources, not level
  • Increase CS with terrain & pressure
